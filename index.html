The Foundation

Ground Rules

01

Be clear.

Start with "It's hard to explain but…" and you've already lost.

02

Put it in the calendar.

No calendar invite = it doesn't exist. Late without notice = you're not serious.

03

Speak up.

Screwed up? Changed course? Say it. Silence kills trust.

04

Trust is an upfront investment.

Given by default — then watched closely. Trust loss is targeted, not generalized.

05

Bottom line up front.

Lead with the conclusion. Context comes second — only if needed.

Format Matters

Communication & Feedback

What Works

  • Bullet points and structured lists
  • Visual formats: charts, diagrams, tables
  • Bottom line up front
  • Clean formatting — it signals respect

What Doesn't

  • Long unstructured text blocks
  • Walls of information
  • Vague conclusions buried at the end
  • Messy docs — "I don't care" signal

Giving feedback: Always privately. Flag the issue, then give space. He needs time to process — absorbs first, then reacts. Worst move: confront publicly or demand instant response.

How We Work

Initiative & Delegation

On Initiative

  • Think before asking
  • Suggest improvements
  • Push things forward on your own
If you burn bright — without burning others — you're in. He mentors. He invests. Not in the quiet ones. In the real ones.

Delegation Protocol

  • Ask questions immediately if unclear
  • Agree on a deadline — no deadline = no task
  • Won't make it? Say it EARLY, not last moment
  • Include: why + new deadline
  • No surprises. Proactive comm = trust maintained

Understanding the Engine

Energy & Rhythm

⬆ Peak Performance

  • Morning, working from home
  • Silence, no interruptions
  • Strategic thinking time
  • Live conversations with strong people

⚡ Motivation Drivers

  • New challenges — energy flows toward the unexplored
  • Conversations with inspiring people

⬇ Energy Killers

When Things Get Tough

Stress & Conflicts

When Stressed

  • Leave him alone — don't push, don't pile on
  • Simple support only: "You'll handle it."
  • Not solutions, not advice — just presence
  • He'll come back when he's processed it
Don't rush. Don't fix. Just be there.

On Conflicts

  • Doesn't avoid them — resolves here and now
  • Can flare up — but cools down
  • Believes conflicts must be resolved fast
  • Same approach in business and personal life
His door is literally open. Knock. Step in. Sort it out.

Self-Awareness

Shadow Sides

He knows these — and actively works on them

Too Fast

Not everyone keeps up.

Can run ahead and not notice others fell behind. Speak up if you need to slow down.

Too Demanding

High standards, always.

"If it's obvious to me, why isn't it obvious to you?" — he's working on calibrating expectations.

Impatient

Needs everything quickly.

Speed is a value, but he knows it can create pressure. Flag it if it's too much.

Perfectionism

The bar is high.

More than control — it's about quality. But he recognizes diminishing returns.

Stubborn

Hard to change once set.

Requires strong data or a completely new angle. Pressure won't work — arguments will.

Can Miss Contributions

Too fast, too much going on.

Doesn't mean he doesn't value it. Speak up — he'll hear you. Stay quiet? You're gone.
